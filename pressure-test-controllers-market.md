Low pressure controllers are designed to handle pressures below 1000 psi, typically used in applications such as water or air testing. On the other hand, high pressure controllers are capable of handling pressures above 1000 psi, commonly used in industries like oil and gas. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring the safety and efficiency of pressure testing processes.</p></p>
