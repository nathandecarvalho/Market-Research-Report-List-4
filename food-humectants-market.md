<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Food humectants are substances that help retain moisture in food products, preventing them from drying out. There are two main types of food humectants: natural and synthetic. Natural humectants, such as glycerin and honey, are derived from plants and animals. They are considered more natural and may be preferred by consumers looking for clean label ingredients. Synthetic humectants, on the other hand, are chemical compounds like propylene glycol and sorbitol. They are often less expensive and more stable than natural alternatives. Both types of humectants play an important role in food preservation and texture enhancement.</p></p>
