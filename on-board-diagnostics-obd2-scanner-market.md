<p><p>On-board diagnostics (OBD)2 scanners are used for diagnosing and monitoring the performance of vehicles. There are three main types of OBD2 scanners: Bluetooth scanners, software-powered scanners, and hand-held scanners. Bluetooth scanners connect wirelessly to a smartphone or tablet, allowing for easy data access and analysis. Software-powered scanners use computer programs to interpret data collected from the vehicle's onboard computer. Hand-held scanners are portable devices that can be plugged directly into the vehicle for immediate diagnostics. Each type offers unique features and benefits for vehicle maintenance and troubleshooting.</p></p>
