<p><p>Centralized vacuum systems come in two main types: pipeline and built-in systems. Pipeline systems consist of a network of pipes installed throughout a building or home, allowing for easy connection of vacuum hoses to clean various areas. Built-in systems, on the other hand, typically consist of a central vacuum unit installed in a utility room or garage, with outlets strategically placed throughout the building for attachment of hoses. Both types offer convenience and efficiency in cleaning spaces, but differ in terms of installation and use.</p></p>
