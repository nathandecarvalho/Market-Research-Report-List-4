<p><p>- Biomass Formation: In microbial EOR, certain microorganisms are used to stimulate the growth of biomass which can help in increasing oil recovery by altering the reservoir rock characteristics.</p><p>- Bio-Surfactants: Microorganisms produce bio-surfactants which can reduce interfacial tension between oil and water, making it easier to extract oil from reservoirs.</p><p>- Bio-Polymers: These are substances produced by microorganisms that can improve the viscosity of water, enhancing oil recovery.</p><p>- Bio-Solvents: Microorganisms produce bio-solvents that can dissolve oil components, making it easier to recover oil.</p><p>- Organic Acids: Microorganisms can produce organic acids that can help in breaking down rock formations and enhancing oil recovery.</p><p>- Bio-Gases: Certain microbial activities can produce bio-gases such as methane, which can help in displacing oil from reservoirs.</p><p>- Bio-emulsifiers: Microorganisms can produce bio-emulsifiers that can stabilize oil-in-water emulsions, making it easier to recover oil.</p><p>- Hydrocarbon Metabolism: Some microorganisms have the ability to metabolize hydrocarbons, which can help in degrading oil components and enhancing oil recovery.</p></p>
