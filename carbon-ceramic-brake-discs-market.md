<p><p>Drilled carbon-ceramic brake discs and slotted carbon-ceramic brake discs are two common types of high-performance brake discs used in the automotive industry. Drilled discs have holes drilled into the surface to dissipate heat and prevent brake fade, while slotted discs have grooves cut into the surface to improve braking performance and reduce brake dust buildup. Both types offer superior braking performance, durability, and resistance to brake fade compared to traditional iron brake discs.</p></p>
