<p><p>Aerospace fasteners are divided into two main types: threaded fasteners and non-threaded fasteners. Threaded fasteners, such as screws and bolts, have spiral grooves and are used to hold together different components by screwing through them. Non-threaded fasteners, on the other hand, do not have spiral grooves and include rivets, pins, and clips. These fasteners are used in aerospace applications to provide mechanical support, join components together, and handle high levels of stress and vibration in aircraft and spacecraft construction.</p></p>
