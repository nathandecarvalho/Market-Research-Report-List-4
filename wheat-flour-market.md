<p><p>The wheat flour market offers various types to cater to different culinary needs. Self-raising flour contains raising agents for baked goods. Bread flour has a higher gluten content for bread making. Biscuit flour is finely milled for tender cookies. Cake flour has a low protein content for light cakes. Whole meal flour includes all parts of the grain for added nutrients. Resultant flour is derived from milling wheat for various purposes. Semolina is coarse flour commonly used in pasta and desserts. Each type serves a unique purpose in the culinary world.</p></p>
