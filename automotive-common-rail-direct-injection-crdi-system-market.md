<p><p>The automotive Common Rail Direct Injection (CRDI) system market consists of two main types: Single Direct Injection (SDI) and Turbocharged Direct Injection (TDI). </p><p>SDI systems involve injecting fuel directly into the combustion chamber of each cylinder without the use of a turbocharger. TDI systems, on the other hand, utilize a turbocharger to compress air before injecting fuel directly into the combustion chamber, resulting in improved fuel efficiency and performance.</p><p>Both SDI and TDI technologies are widely used in modern diesel engines to enhance power output and reduce emissions.</p></p>
