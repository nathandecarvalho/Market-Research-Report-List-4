<p><p>Fire detection equipment can be divided into two main types based on their communication methods - wired and wireless. Wired systems require physical connections between the various components of the system, such as detectors, control panels, and alarm devices. On the other hand, wireless systems use radio frequency signals to communicate between the components without the need for physical connections. Both types have their own advantages and disadvantages, such as cost, installation complexity, and reliability, which influence their respective market shares and adoption rates.</p></p>
