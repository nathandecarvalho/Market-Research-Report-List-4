<p><p>Forged crankshafts can be classified into two main types: Integral and Assembled. Integral crankshafts are made from a single piece of metal, resulting in a stronger and more durable component. On the other hand, Assembled crankshafts are made by joining multiple pieces together, typically resulting in a lighter and more cost-effective option. Integral crankshafts are often used in high-performance engines where strength and reliability are crucial, while Assembled crankshafts are commonly found in less demanding applications. Both types cater to different market needs based on performance and cost considerations.</p></p>
