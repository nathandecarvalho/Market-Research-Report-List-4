<p><ul><li>Polyvinylidene Fluoride</li><li>Polytetrafluoroethylene</li><li>Perfluoroethylene Propylene Copolymer</li><li>Ethylene-Tetrafluoroethylene Copolymer</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Fluoropolymer coatings are divided into different types based on the specific polymer used in the coating. The main types include Polyvinylidene Fluoride (PVDF), Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), Perfluoroethylene Propylene Copolymer (FEP), Ethylene-Tetrafluoroethylene Copolymer (ETFE), and other variations. PVDF coatings are known for their durability and resistance to harsh chemicals and UV rays. PTFE coatings offer low friction properties and high temperature resistance. FEP coatings are valued for their non-stick properties, while ETFE coatings provide excellent weather resistance. Other variations in fluoropolymer coatings offer a range of specific properties for various applications.</p></p>
