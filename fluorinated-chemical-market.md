<p><p>Fluorinated chemicals can be categorized into two main types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic fluorinated chemicals typically consist of metal fluorides, such as sodium fluoride and calcium fluoride, while organic fluorinated chemicals contain carbon-fluorine bonds and are commonly found in products like Teflon and pharmaceuticals. Inorganic fluorinated chemicals are often used in industries like metallurgy and glass manufacturing, while organic fluorinated chemicals are prevalent in the production of non-stick coatings, refrigerants, and fire retardants. Both types play important roles in various sectors of the economy.</p></p>
