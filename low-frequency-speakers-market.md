<p><p>Low frequency speakers are divided into three main market types based on their size and frequency range: woofers, midwoofers, and subwoofers. Woofers are large speakers designed to reproduce deep bass frequencies, typically ranging from 40Hz to 1kHz. Midwoofers are slightly smaller and handle mid-range frequencies, typically from 100Hz to 2kHz. Subwoofers are the largest and most powerful of the three, specializing in ultra-low frequencies below 100Hz. Each market type caters to different audio needs and preferences.</p></p>
